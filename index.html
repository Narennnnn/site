my story

I'm from Ayodhya — fluent in Awadhi, Hindi, and professional English. Growing up, I watched my cousins do incredible things on our home PC, and that's when something clicked. The idea that a machine could be so powerful, so versatile — it sparked a curiosity that never really went away. The more I learned and built, the more fascinating this field became. Even now, every day teaches me something new.

I've always been the studious-but-outgoing type. School topper in 12th, but also the guy leading NCC cadets as Senior Under Officer, organizing social activities, and making sure things never got boring. That balance of discipline and fun has stayed with me.

What excites me about engineering? A new problem to solve every day. The chance to fix yesterday's mistakes and build something better today. I thrive in environments with challenging problems and real opportunities to learn and grow.

My first role was at Tonnochy Capital, a Hong Kong-based high-frequency trading startup — REST APIs, WebSockets, real-time data at scale. Milliseconds mattered. I learned to build clean, efficient, fault-tolerant systems. Now at Vela (formerly Reewild), I've taken ownership of projects like the Developer Portal, API Documentation, and Email Receipt Radar (secured Google's approval). Every project is an opportunity to combine technical precision with user experience.

Outside of work, running has become a big part of my life. Completed my first half marathon in 2:24 hours — now training for a full marathon by the end of 2026. There's something about endurance sports that mirrors engineering: show up consistently, embrace the discomfort, trust the process. I'm also adventurous — recently explored Konkan, soaking in local Marathi culture, cuisine, and learning how people live differently.

When I'm not running or coding, you'll find me with coffee, good music, or a great movie. I play cricket, 8-ball pool, and snooker to unwind. I believe the best teams thrive on trust, open communication, and a sense of fun — and I bring that energy to every collaboration.
